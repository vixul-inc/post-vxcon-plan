FG 1 + FG 2 high level summary
From Vixul page To Public — team reposts Afternoon — 2pm CST ⚡ Marketing team
›
Both focus groups in one post. Sharp conclusions, not summaries. Designed to make people wish they were there.
Draft post
Two focus groups. Six things the room concluded at VixulCon. On unlocking margin with AI: 1. AI margin is an account-level metric, not a project-level one. The lift shows up across the account — faster delivery, more projects, same team. Measuring project-by-project is why firms think AI isn't paying off when it actually is. 2. The biggest threat to AI-services margin isn't cost. It's the buyer's mental model. The moment a client hears "AI," they assume fast and cheap. That perception tax caps your pricing power more than competition ever will. 3. AI has already floor-priced the routine work. The remaining margin lives in judgment — exactly where AI still struggles. Firms that can't price judgment won't capture the upside. On pricing AI-first services: 4. Distribution is the new bottleneck. Delivery isn't. AI has made delivery fast enough that one customer at a time is officially too slow. The constraint moved. Most firms haven't restructured for it. 5. The pricing problem is usually a positioning problem in disguise. You can't charge outcome-based rates if your service isn't narrow enough to measure outcomes. 6. AI-first services aren't competing for the software budget. They're competing for the labor budget — a structurally bigger pool. That's why aggressive AI-services pricing still works. This is what 140 people in a room in Austin concluded on Monday. #ServiceAsSoftware #AIServices #VixulCon

Theme 1 — Building the AI-First Foundation
From Vixul page To Public — team reposts Morning — 9am CST ⚡ Marketing team
›
First of 4 theme posts. Provocation-first. References VixulCon talks without being a recap.
Draft post
At VixulCon, three talks tackled the same foundational question: what does it actually mean to build an AI-first services company — not just use AI in one? Most services companies are using AI to do what they already do, faster. That'll improve margins short-term. But the companies that will be worth acquiring in 3 years aren't optimizing their current delivery model with AI. They're rebuilding what delivery means. The difference shows up in how you staff, how you price, and whether your clients can ever outgrow you. Three VixulCon sessions went deep on this: Aater Suleman (Vixul) on building AI-first services companies — customization at scale only works when delivery velocity and margins move together. Uttam Kumaran & Robert Tseng (Brainforge AI) on building an AI-native agency — when AI is the foundation, your staffing model, pricing, and client expectations all need rebuilding from scratch. Mannan Amin (Tintash) on why AI transformation fails without Workflow-User Fit — without it, AI investment is theatre. The through-line: if everyone rebuilds on AI, what's actually defensible? The room didn't fully answer that. Neither has anyone else — yet. #ServiceAsSoftware #AIServices #VixulCon

Theme 3 — New Markets, New Delivery Models
From Vixul page To Public — team reposts Morning — 9am CST ⚡ Marketing team
›
Weekend post. No email. The 'new markets' angle travels well — accessible to wider LI audience.
Draft post
The fastest-growing opportunities for AI services aren't in tech companies. They're in sectors that have never been able to afford custom software — until now. Three talks at VixulCon made this concrete: Ryan Palm (Techment) on modernizing US government property tax and land records systems — replacing legacy software with cloud-native SaaS and AI-driven valuations. The insight: it's not a tech upgrade. It's a citizen experience transformation that creates long-term stickiness. Thiago Maior (EZOps Cloud) on the AI-first DevOps agency. When AI runs ops, engineers steer strategy. That's not a productivity story — it's a talent positioning story. Javeed Muhammad (Bitsol) on prototype-led development in healthcare. In regulated industries, compliance baked in at prototype stage isn't a constraint — it's the competitive advantage. The pattern across all three: the moat isn't the AI. It's the domain knowledge that makes the AI trustworthy in high-stakes contexts. #ServiceAsSoftware #AIServices #VixulCon

The question VixulCon didn't answer
From Vixul page To Public — team reposts Morning — 9am CST ⚡ Marketing team
›
Intellectually honest. Surfaces unresolved threads. Very shareable — founders will tag each other.
Draft post
VixulCon had a lot of sharp conclusions. But there were a few questions the room raised — and then the conversation moved on before anyone could finish them. The ones that stayed with me: If AI commoditizes routine delivery, and the margin lives in judgment — how do you hire for judgment at scale? Nobody had a framework for this. If distribution is now the bottleneck, not delivery — what does a services company's go-to-market actually look like in 2026? The old playbooks don't fit. If buyers are confused about who to trust with AI — what does "building in public" actually mean for a services firm? Not a SaaS company. A services firm. These aren't rhetorical questions.
